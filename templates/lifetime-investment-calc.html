Lifetime Investment Calculator

A multi-era investment calculator for the different stages of your life.

Repository

Background

This calculator was built around the time I got my first real job and needed to set up a reasonable budget. I wasn't used to having discretionary funds, and knew I needed to make some decisions about how much to save and invest. It seemed reasonable to me that my investment and expenditure habits were going to change over time, and I wanted to be able to quickly illustrate what the outcome of those different periods would be.

How to Use

This calculator allows you specify subsequent periods of investment (and expenditure) which differ from each other. Many calculators allow you to input one set of parameters and view the growth of the investment over time, but few allow you to display changes in investing strategy over time.

For example, in the beginning of your investing journey you may experiment with higher volatility, and average a return of 10% per year over the course of 25 years. You may then change your strategy to invest more safely for 5 years, returning an average of 8% each year. You may then decide to retire, moving your money into more guaranteed accounts which return less, maybe 4.5% annually, while at the same time withdrawing money each month to live off of. This calculator allows you to input each of those, and see what the outcome will be.
